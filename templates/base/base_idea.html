Idea Details

Video: Billing Beyond the Rig

Video was created by Alpacalypse Productions, http://alpacabomb.com

Purchase and Rental Management. On the web.

Apollo's purpose is to demonstrate advantages that a web based billing and authentication management application would have over a rig based solution.

Currently, our customers request equipment from Pason. This request goes to a field technician who delivers the equipment to the rig. The field technician uses our rig site billing software to add a pending charge for the equipment. The on site driller approves this charge. Finally, the billing software enables the equipment.

This current workflow raises some concerns:

  • Why are we sending billing data back and forth between the rig?

    Rather than have the rigs all download a version of the price list, we should have all prices centralized and consistent across the field. Rigs should not have to download the price list, part list, field tech credentials, etc. Rigs should listen to our central servers to see what equipment is active. Rigs should keep this state in case internet connectivity goes down, but still update itself whenever a change is made on the central server.

  • Why does control of charges rely on a driller's approval instead of the head office/corporate approval?

    The corporate office user should be responsible for which charges are purchased. Drillers typically don't care about which charges are active, so long as the equipment is enabled. Our existing workflow causes some churn within our customers. There is frequently disputes as to what is paid for and what isn't. Having an easily accessible purchase/rental management solution would fix this problem.

  • Why do field technicians need to go to a rig to manage charges?

    Field technicians should be able to manage purchases and equipment from their mobile devices and computers without having to travel to a rig. Making a field technician drive out to a rig is expensive. All unnecessary traveling should be minimized. Having a web based billing application mitigates field technicians traveling due to billing errors.

  • How do we expand our current billing model to accommodate off rig products?

    Currently, our billing software exists on the rig. This makes it difficult for off rig innovations to tie into our existing billing workflow. To keep our unique process of billing and equipment activation, a solution is to use a centralized server. This will allow any product with internet capability to tie into our billing model. This is a more scalable solution than having all of our billing clustered among each rig.

This prototype is not meant to be a drop in replacement for our current billing software. It is simply a way to show what a web application to manage rig rentals and purchases could look like. It is not tied to DataHub or any rigs. Although the server does support websockets, I did not implement a websocket handler for our activated equipment.

Moreover, I made many simplifications on our existing price list and charge list models. I scrapped the idea of having pending, toggled, and approved charges. I broke down each individual charge model such that there are three distinct methods of billing:

  • Activity Based Charges: Charges based on an arbitrary unit of activity (for example billing per MB for new satellite payment methods).
  • Time Based Charges: Charges based on lengths of time elapsed, such as our typical SPUD time to Release time charges.
  • Unit Based Charges: Charges with one time occurrence, like a delivery or installation fee.

I introduced additional state to each of our stations (whether it is a rig, or an office). Each station now supports awareness as to what equipment needs to be delivered by a field technician, as well as what already exists on site.

Summary

I hope to raise attention to what a unified web based billing process could offer to Pason. I think that if a re-write were to occur, we should consider moving billing beyond the drilling rig and into the web.

Alexander Wong
Software Developer Intern (EDR)
Pason Systems Corp.
